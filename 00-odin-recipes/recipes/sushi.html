Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish consisting of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets stacked with rich, savory ingredients. Each layer typically includes a hearty meat sauce or a vegetarian filling, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella.

Baked until bubbly and golden, lasagna offers a comforting blend of flavors and textures, making it a beloved and satisfying meal for any occasion.

Ingredient List

  • Meat
  • Onion and garlic
  • Tomato products
  • Sguar
  • Spices and seasonings
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Cheeses
  • Egg

Steps for Making Lasagna

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the meat sauce:
    • In a large skillet, cook ground beef or sausage over medium heat until browned.
    • Add onions, garlic, and cook until softened.
    • Stir in tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, and your favorite herbs and spices (e.g., basil, oregano, salt, and pepper).
    • Simmer for 20-30 minutes to develop flavors.
  3. Prepare the cheese mixture:
    • In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg and some grated Parmesan cheese.
    • Add chopped fresh herbs (e.g., parsley) if desired.
  4. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  5. Assemble the lasagna:
    • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
    • Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
    • Spread a layer of the cheese mixture over the noodles.
    • Add another layer of meat sauce over the cheese.
    • Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
    • Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of mozzarella cheese.
  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  7. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  8. Let the lasagna cool for 10-15 minutes before serving to allow it to set.
